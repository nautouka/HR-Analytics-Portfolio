Project Narrative & Strategic Insights

Through Power BI, I developed a dynamic HR intelligence system analyzing workforce data from 2006 to 2019. Using interactive year-slicers, stakeholders can seamlessly monitor labor costs, performance distribution, and retention risks across different fiscal periods.

Taking 2016 as a representative case study (221 staff), the following diagnostic insights were identified:

1. Workforce & Compensation Dynamics

  • Departmental Focus: The Production department remains the core unit, accounting for 64.25% (142 staff) of the headcount.
  • Salary Insights: While the company average is $69.32K, a significant gap exists between Executive levels ($250K) and Production ($59.9K). This reflects a typical organizational structure with a single executive role.
  • Pay Equity: Preliminary analysis of gender pay distribution shows no immediately visible disparity; however, further analysis at the role and grade level is recommended to validate pay equity comprehensively.

2. Retention & High-Performer Attrition

  • Attrition Warning: The Production department recorded the highest turnover rate at 12.68%, followed by IT/IS at 11.11%.
  • "Brain Drain": Among terminated employees in Production, a notable proportion were high performers. Employees rated as "Exceeds" accounted for 14.29% of Production department exits.
  • Root Cause: Exit drivers are primarily linked to "Unhappiness" and "External Opportunities" rather than salary alone, based on termination reason data.

3. Performance & Predictive Sentiment

  • Leading Indicators: Employee satisfaction scores below 3.0 show a strong correlation with future PIP (Performance Improvement Plan) status, suggesting that low engagement is a reliable leading indicator of performance decline.
  • Predictive Risk: Low sentiment can serve as an early warning signal for productivity deterioration, enabling proactive intervention before performance issues escalate.

4. Recruitment ROI & Operational Risk

  • Effective Channels: Based on preliminary cross-analysis, Employee Referrals tend to yield candidates with higher average salaries ($78.2K) and demonstrate lower turnover rates compared to other sources, indicating strong candidate quality and cultural fit.
  • Attendance Risk: Several employees identified as "High Absence Risk" have reached 20 days of absence, which correlates with a 33.33% turnover rate among this group—signaling potential burnout or imminent resignation.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Implement Stay Interviews for high-performing staff in Production to address cultural dissatisfaction and understand retention drivers beyond compensation.
  • Reallocate Recruitment Budget from high-churn job boards to Internal Referral Incentive programs, leveraging the demonstrated ROI of employee referrals.
  • Establish a "Critical Satisfaction Threshold" at 3.5 to trigger proactive HR counseling and intervention, enabling early detection and support for employees at risk of disengagement or performance decline.
